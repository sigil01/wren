TESTIMONY

The wren was singing. That was the whole crime.
The wran, the wran, the king of all birds,
St. Stephen's Day he was caught in the furze;
Although he is little his family is great;
Stand up old lady and give us a trate.
— Traditional, Ireland

I. The Bush
A wren in a hedge. Cold morning. The bird sings because that's the arrangement between wrens and mornings. No audience. No intent. Just the sound coming out the way water comes out of alchemilla — from within.

II. The Hunt
Drums. Boys with sticks. December 26. The procession approaches. Rhythm multiplies. The singing gets smaller. The singing stops.
2:30–3:30
III. The Silence
Two thousand years compressed into wind through empty hedge. The absence where the wren was. A low hum like the earth remembering something it can't name.
3:30–4:30
IV. The Song Outlasts
Morning again. The wren sings the same song. Not triumphant — just present. Because it's morning and that's what wrens do. The punishment outlasted the memory of why. The song outlasted the punishment.
